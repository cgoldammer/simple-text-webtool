What is this?

This is a simple dashboard that lets you use predictive text models.

Predictive models with quantitative data are very popular: They can find out what you'll buy, who you'll fall in love with, or when your milk goes bad. But building predictive models from text seems hard.

Not true! At least, it's not true if you're willing to start simple. Here, I assume that the most popular words in the text predict the outcome (called bag-of-words).

This results in a large number of flags. I then use a ridge regression (from the scikit-learn package) to predict the outcome.

I'm happy for any feedback. I'm currently working on adding the following:

  • Better data cleaning: Currently, the word "don't" is split into "don" and "t".
  • Lemmatization: Currently, "likes" and "like" will be counted as the same word - I'll probably use textblob to lemmatize the original text.
  • Additional features: I'm planning to start by incorporating sentence structure and complexity.
  • Additional models: Let me know if you have good data on blog comments. I would love to investigate Godwin's law empirically.

My thanks go to Ryan Wang who showed me how to estimate these models easily. Also, I use some of his code!
